Segregation simulator

A simple agent-based model of racial segregation drawn from Thomas Schelling's work and Allen B. Downey's book "Think Complexity. Blue and red tiles are occupied, and at each step we pick a random occupied tile. We check the 8 "neighbour" cells around the tile, and if the number of neighbours of the same colour is less than a certain threshold, that agent moves to a random unoccupied space. These random moves can produce sharply differentiated areas of the two colours.
